## What we collect

If you create an account, we store the email address you sign up with, your hashed password
(handled by our auth provider — we never see your plain password), and the profile fields you
fill in during onboarding: your role (patient, loved one, or exploring), the disease you've
chosen to focus on, and an optional display name. We also store any pages you've saved.

We log basic technical information about requests (IP address, user agent) for security and
to keep the site running. We do not use third-party advertising trackers.

## How we use it

Account data is used only to personalize the site for you — to surface the disease pages,
mechanisms, and interventions relevant to your focus, and to remember what you've saved. We
do not sell your data. We do not share it with third parties for marketing.

## Security

Authentication and data storage are handled by Supabase, a reputable cloud provider. We use
row-level security so your data is only accessible to you.

## Your rights

You can update your profile or delete your account anytime from your account page. Deleting
your account removes your profile and saved items from our database.

## Children

Axonome is not directed to children under 13. We do not knowingly collect data from children.
