Finding better and less invasive biomarkers for neurodegenerative diseases is crucial to detecting disease earlier, tracking progression more thoroughly, and assessing how well therapies work. This study identified brain-derived tau (BD-tau) in blood as a new biomarker for tracking [ischemic stroke](https://www.axonome.org/ischemic-stroke) progression and severity. BD-tau levels in blood correlated with infarct volume (volume of tissue that died from lack of oxygen). Higher levels also were associated with worse 3-month, 12-month, and 36-month outcomes, more so than CT scans of the infarct area in fact. This shows BD-tau could be one of the most valuable markers doctors can look at during a stroke.
