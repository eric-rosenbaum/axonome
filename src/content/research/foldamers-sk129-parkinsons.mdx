title: "Foldamers rescue synucleinopathy phenotypes in multiple in vitro and in vivo models"

summary: "The synthetic foldamer SK-129 binds alpha-synuclein aggregates, reduces their spread via exosomes, and improved survival in a mouse Parkinson's model."
---

Foldamers are synthetic molecules that spontaneously fold into specific shapes when in solution. Here, the authors used a foldamer called SK-129 to bind to [alpha-synuclein aggregates](https://www.axonome.org/protein-aggregation). In [Parkinson's](https://www.axonome.org/parkinsons) models of both worms and cultured human neurons, treatment with SK-129 improved many hallmarks of disease including number of dopaminergic neurons and [ROS levels](https://www.axonome.org/oxidative-stress). In cells, alpha-synuclein aggregates from diseased cells can spread to healthy cells in sacs called exosomes. When cells were treated with SK-129, exosomes contained fewer aggregates, and the spread of aggregation was lowered. Finally, the authors treated a mouse Parkinson's model with SK-129, and they found that the treated mice survived and retained their body weight, while the untreated mice did not.
