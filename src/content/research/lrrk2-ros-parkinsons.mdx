summary: "Disease-associated LRRK2 mutations drive oxidative stress in Parkinson's models by over-activating NADPH oxidase 2 (NOX2), an effect blocked by LRRK2 kinase inhibitors."
---

[Oxidative stress](https://www.axonome.org/oxidative-stress) has been implicated in [Parkinson's](https://www.axonome.org/parkinsons) pathogenesis, but where it comes from and how it is regulated remains an open question. Mutations in leucine rich repeat kinase 2 are associated with PD. Overexpression of mutant [LRRK2](https://www.axonome.org/lrrk-2) is associated with oxidative stress, and this toxicity can be rescued with antioxidants. Elevated LRRK2 activity from disease-associated mutations is associated with more ROS and lipid peroxidation, and this seems to happen by over activating a protein called NADPH oxidase 2 (NOX2). Inhibitors of LRRK2 kinase blocks this increase in ROS. Rotenone, a [mitochondrial toxin](https://www.axonome.org/mitochondrial-dysfunction), induced oxidative stress is fixed when LRRK2 kinase is inhibited or when LRRK2 was knocked out. In models of PD, LRRK2 kinase inhibition prevented lipid peroxidation and NOX2 activation.
