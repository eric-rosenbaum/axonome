summary: "MEK inhibition by the cancer drug trametinib selectively damages peripheral neurons by reducing NMNAT2 expression, which can be rescued by overexpressing NMNAT2."
---

The MAPK pathway, of which MEK and ERK are members, is important for signaling and regulating which genes are expressed. Trametinib is a MEK inhibitor used to treat cancer, but it causes lesions and [degeneration in neurons outside of the brain and spinal cord (peripheral nerves)](https://www.axonome.org/cipn). Interestingly, nerves from the spinal cord and brain (the central nervous system) seemed to be protected by trametinib, showing that context is really important for determining the effects of stress on neuron health. The authors found that when peripheral nerves were treated with trametinib, their expression of the protein NMNAT2 was significantly decreased. NMNAT2 is important because it helps make NAD+, which is necessary for cellular metabolism and for preventing [SARM1 activation](https://www.axonome.org/sar-mopathy). By overexpressing NMNAT2, the authors were able to reverse the neurodegenerative effects of trametinib.
