summary: "Single-nucleus RNA sequencing of 67 post-mortem brains reveals that SEMA6D-TREM2 signaling between neurons and microglia is disrupted in Alzheimer's, impairing amyloid-beta clearance."
---

The authors isolated nuclei from 67 post-mortem brains and then sequenced the RNA from them. From this, they were able to predict thousands of cell-cell communication interactions, and found that there was extensive communication between neurons and microglia (the resident immune cells of the brain). This communication appeared to be disrupted in the brains of Alzheimer's patients. They showed that [TREM2](https://www.axonome.org/trem-2) is a hub for this communication, and that a protein called SEMA6D is a key signaling partner for the activation of microglia. This communication is helpful for clearing amyloid-beta plaques, so its disruption in [Alzheimer's](https://www.axonome.org/alzheimers) helps explain the build up of plaques seen in patients.
